Research at Norchester

Advancing knowledge through collaboration, curiosity, and rigorous inquiry

Our Research Mission

Norchester University has built a research culture from the ground up — one that prizes interdisciplinary work, mentorship of undergraduate researchers, and real-world impact. Since our founding in 2019, our faculty have secured over $4.2 million in external funding and published in leading peer-reviewed venues across disciplines.

Our small size is a strength. Faculty know their students. Collaboration happens across hallways, not just across departments.

Open Call: The Office of Research is accepting proposals for the 2026 Internal Seed Grant program. Awards of up to $15,000 available. Deadline: May 1, 2026.

About Norchester University

A young institution with a clear sense of purpose

Our Story

Norchester University was founded in 2019 by a coalition of researchers, educators, and community leaders who believed that New Hampshire deserved a research university built for the 21st century — one that didn't inherit the inertia of older institutions.

From day one, we made deliberate choices: small cohort sizes, no graduate student teaching assistants teaching core courses, a mandatory interdisciplinary core curriculum, and a commitment to placing every undergraduate in a mentored research or practicum experience before graduation.

Five years in, with 2,340 students, 180 faculty, and our first doctoral graduates, we remain committed to proving that a young institution can compete on quality of ideas.

"We built Norchester to answer a question: what does a university look like when you design it from scratch, knowing what we know now?"
— Dr. Celeste Okafor, Founding President
